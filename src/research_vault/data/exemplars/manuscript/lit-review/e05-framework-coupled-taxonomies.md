We begin the review with several highly visible real-world cases of where unfair machine learning algorithms have led to suboptimal and discriminatory outcomes in Section 2. In Section 3, we describe the different types and sources of biases that occur within the data-algorithms-users loop mentioned above. Next, in Section 4, we present the different ways that the concept of fairness has been operationalized and studied in the literature. We discuss the ways in which these two concepts are coupled.
